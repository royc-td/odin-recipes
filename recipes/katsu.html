Chicken Katsu

Home

Description

This recipe combines a rich curry sauce with a cripsy, fried chicken cutlet. Serve with steamed rice, carrots and potatoes.

Ingredients

Curry Sauce:

  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 2 medium onions, chopped
  • 3 medium potatoes, chopped
  • 2 medium carrots, chopped
  • 3 1/2 cups water, or as needed
  • 1 container curry sauce mix (such as S&B Golden Curry)li>
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire Sauce
  • 1 tablespoon apricot jam
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder

    • Chicken Katsu:

  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves
  • kosher salt and ground
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 large egg, beaten, or as needed
  • 1 cup panko bread crumbs
  • 1/2 cup peanut oil for frying

    • Steps

    Step 1

    Make the curry sauce: Heat the oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Cook onions in hot oil until soft and starting to caramelize, about 8 minutes. Add potatoes and carrots. Pour in water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and cook until potatoes are soft - about 45 minutes

    Step 2

    Remove from heat and stir in curry sauce mix until dissolved. Add ketchup, Worcestershire sauce, jam, and soy sauce. Simmer until sauce is thickened, 10 to 15 minutes.

    Step 3

    Meanwhile, make the chicken katsu: Flatten chicken breasts with a kitchen mallet to a thickness of 1/4 inch. Season with salt and pepper.

    Step 4

    Place flour onto a large plate, add beaten egg to a small plate, and place bread crumbs onto a second large plate.

    Step 5

    Press chicken into flour to coat, dip in beaten egg, and press in bread crumbs to coat generously.

    Step 6

    Heat oil in a large saucepan to 180 degrees C. Carefully lower breaded cutlets into hot oil and fry until golden brown, about 3 minutes per side. An instant-read thermometer inserted into a cutlet should read 75 degrees C

    Step 7

    While chicken is cooking, stir curry powder into sauce until incorporated

    Step 8

    Slice chicken into strips and spoon curry sauce over the top to serve.
